Manufacturing Process

At DM GLASS, we follow modern and precise manufacturing techniques to produce high-quality glass products that meet international safety standards.

Toughened Glass

1. Cutting: Glass sheets are cut to the desired size.
2. Edge Polishing: Smooth edges to prevent cracks.
3. Heating: Glass is heated to around 700Â°C.
4. Rapid Cooling: Glass is cooled quickly using high-pressure air jets to increase strength.
5. Quality Check: Inspect for defects and stress points.

Laminated Glass

1. Cut glass layers to size.
2. Place a PVB or EVA interlayer between glass sheets.
3. Heat and press in an autoclave to bond layers.
4. Inspect for clarity and safety standards.

DGU (Double Glazed Unit)

1. Two glass panes are cut to size.
2. Spacer bars filled with desiccant are added.
3. Panes are sealed to create an airtight space.
4. Inspect for insulation performance.

Frosted Glass

1. Cut glass to desired dimensions.
2. Apply acid etching, sandblasting, or film to create frosted patterns.
3. Clean and inspect surface quality.
4. Can combine with laminated or toughened glass for extra strength.

Fluted, Colored & Design Glass

1. Use textured rollers or molds to create fluted or patterned surface.
2. Apply color coating or tinted layer as required.
3. Heat-treat or toughen if needed.
4. Inspect for uniformity and quality.
